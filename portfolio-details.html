    Your discussion could consider the following: the economic benefits to companies, the implications of a breach of regulations and laws such as reputational damage and trust and business competitiveness. Identify a typical global company and describe the nature of the business to set the context for the discussion.

    The Internet of Things (IoT) is a concept that is used to describe technology that is embedded in items enabling connectivity to the internet (Burgess, 2019). In 2018 there were an estimated 7 billion IoT devices connected to the internet with an expectation that by 2025 it will have grown to 75 billion (Maayan, 2020). Each one of the IoT connections is a possible access point for malicious intent and therefore needs to be protected by Cyber Security. The right to privacy was enshrined in the Declaration of Human Rights in 1948 (UNGA, 1948). With the onset of the digital age, those privacy rights have not changed. A company now has a legal obligation to ensure an individual's privacy is protected as defined by the General Data Protection Regulation (Data Protection Act, 2018). In 2018 British Airways (BA) was the subject of a cyber attack which was only announced when a third party revealed the attack had taken place two months later (ICO, 2020). BA was found to be in breach of the EU GDPR regulations by not doing enough to safeguard their customers' personal and financial details. This led to over 400,000 client details accessed and a record fine of Â£20M being levied against BA (ICO, 2020). As well as the financial impact BA has suffered a reputational impact as customers become more aware of their right to have their information protected at all times.
