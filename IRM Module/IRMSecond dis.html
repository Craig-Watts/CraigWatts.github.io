Identify and explain the architecture, structure and functionality of basic components of a computer system, considering Cyber Security issues Evaluate critically implications of the key vulnerabilities and threats of software and network security and approaches to mitigate these issues

Two security technologies that can be employed are the firewall and the encryption of data. Since the inception of the wide-area network (WAN), the firewall has been synonymous with security, whether that WAN was corporate or the internet. The firewall is a simple barrier between the internal systems and the external data traffic trying to reach them. Before the advent of TCP/IP, traffic utilised broadcast protocols that travelled everywhere and could only be blocked or allowed. With the advent of the TCP/IP protocol transmitted in 'packets' of data, the data traffic could be interrogated inline; the firewall would allow or block subject to a ruleset if followed. As the traffic requirements grew, so did the firewalls examining the packets faster and more intelligently. The firewall has developed further and, with the emergence of cloud technologies, has moved from a physical device to a web application firewall (WAF) to meet the growing demands of the consumer better The transmission of information and keeping it secure or secret has been an ongoing goal since before the inception of writing. With the written word and then the transmitted word, there has always been a need to keep the information private. Information can be broken up into data and then encrypted to protect the confidentiality of the information. When I first entered the technological world, data was encrypted inline and offline for transit but not at rest as considered safe stored in a secure building. Data in transit and Data at rest now need to be encrypted to secure the information within every point of a system to ensure the information's integrity. . (Accessed 8th Mar. 2021)
